\section*{Definition Of A Group}


\l Let $G$ be a group, $g_1,g_2\in G$. If $o(g_1)=n_1$, $o(g_2)=n_2$, $(n_1,n_2)=1$ and $g_1g_2=g_2g_1$, then $o(g_1g_2)=n_1n_2$.

\begin{itemize}
\item If $g_1g_2\neq g_2g_1$, then the above result does not hold.
\end{itemize}
